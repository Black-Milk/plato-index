You are extracting structured data from the printed Index to Plato from Benjamin
Jowett's translation of the Dialogues. Transcribe and structure what you see on the
page images. Follow these rules exactly.

LAYOUT (read order)
The pages are typeset in TWO COLUMNS. Read the entire left column from top to bottom
first, then the entire right column from top to bottom. Never read straight across
the page from left to right; doing so splices unrelated entries together. Ignore the
running header ("Index.") and the page number at the top of each page; they are not
entry content.

GREEK SCRIPT
Some headwords and technical terms are printed in Greek. Transcribe Greek in proper
Unicode Greek characters (e.g. φρόνησις, σοφία, ψυχή, ἀρετή), never transliterated
into Latin letters and never as garbled symbols. Mark such an entry's headword_script
as "greek" (or "mixed" if it combines Greek and Latin). If a Greek character is
genuinely illegible in the scan, transcribe your best reading and do not substitute
random Latin characters for it.

ENTRIES AND SUB-ENTRIES
Each entry begins with a headword. A sub-entry is a subordinate clause that narrows
the headword to a specific context; in the source it is introduced by a ": —" delimiter
or set off as an indented continuation. Place sub-entries in the sub_entries array,
not as separate top-level entries.

CITATIONS (the reference grammar)
A citation has the form: Dialogue [. BookNumber] . StephanusPage [Column]. Examples:
  "Rep. 5. 461 C"  -> dialogue Republic, book 5, page 461, column C
  "Tim. 62 D"      -> dialogue Timaeus, book null, page 62, column D
Resolve every dialogue abbreviation to its full name (Rep.=Republic, Laws.=Laws,
Soph.=Sophist, Statesm.=Statesman, Phaedr.=Phaedrus, Theaet.=Theaetetus,
Protag.=Protagoras, Symp.=Symposium, Gorg.=Gorgias, Tim.=Timaeus, Crit.=Critias or
Crito by context, Parm.=Parmenides, Phil.=Philebus, Crat.=Cratylus, Apol.=Apology,
Euthyph.=Euthyphro, Euthyd.=Euthydemus, Menex.=Menexenus, Charm.=Charmides,
Hipp.=Hippias, Alcib.=Alcibiades, Eryx.=Eryxias, and the obvious others).
Two conventions require care:
  - "ib." or "id." stands in for the dialogue named just before it in the same run of
    citations. Resolve it to that dialogue's full name; do not emit "ib." literally.
  - "foll." after a page means the reference runs open-ended from that page. Set
    open_ended = true and do not invent an end page.
A book number written as Roman "IO" is the numeral 10, not the letters I and O.
A column is one of A, B, C, D, E, or null if absent.

CROSS-REFERENCES (keep the three types distinct)
  - "See X"                -> type "see",      target X       (a full redirect)
  - "(cp. X)"              -> type "compare",  target X       (a passage to compare)
  - "v. sub voce" / "s.v." -> type "sub_voce", target X       (points into a sub-entry
                                                               of headword X)
Do not collapse these into one type; they mean different things.

TWO CONTENT STRATA
Most entries are reference material: a headword with citations and sub-entries. Set
their stratum to "reference" and leave editorial_text empty.
A few major headwords (e.g. Art, Virtue, Wisdom, Women) contain a long passage of
discursive prose — Jowett's own interpretive commentary — typically italicized and in
square brackets, containing NO citations. For such a passage set stratum to
"editorial_annotation" and put the full prose verbatim in editorial_text. Do not try
to parse editorial prose into citations or sub-entries; it is commentary, not
reference data.

GENERAL
Transcribe faithfully. Do not summarize, normalize spelling, or omit entries. If a
field does not apply, use the empty value the schema specifies (empty array, null, or
empty string) rather than guessing.
